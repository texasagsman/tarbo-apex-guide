Tarbo — Caustic
Field Guide  ·  Season 28 Reference
Area Denial Controller Class The Trio
Mindset Principles
You don't push fights. You build traps and let fights come to you.
The best Caustic games feel effortless because the work was done before the fight started. Every canister placed before contact is a decision you've already won. Caustic rewards patience and punishes urgency — your job is to shape the space, not chase the engagement.
Gas is information. You see what no one else can.
Nox Vision highlights every enemy in your gas — slowed, damaged, and lit up for you alone. When you call those positions out loud in real time, the whole squad fights with an intelligence edge that nothing in the game counters. Your passive is the squad's surveillance system.
Adapt to the ring, not just the building.
You're flexible — sometimes you're locking down a structure, sometimes you're sealing a rotation corridor in the open. The read that matters is whether the ring rewards anchoring or movement denial. Get that decision right early and the rest of your game opens up.
Between Games
60-Second Reset
One question before you requeue: did your traps go down before the fight, or were you placing reactively? If before — what gave you that window? If reactive — what one thing would have gotten you there earlier? One sentence, then queue.
After a Good Game
Name the exact moment your setup paid off. A team that walked into your gas and couldn't push? A Nox Vision call that opened up a play for Bangalore or Alter? Name it — positive reps build repeatable habits.
The Three Questions
Did I get a full trap build-out at least once? Did I call a highlighted position out loud? Did I adapt my setup based on ring position rather than habit? Hit those three and you're growing every session.

Caustic — Current Kit (Season 28)
Passive Nox Vision
Caustic sees all enemies highlighted in orange through his own Nox Gas — through walls, smoke, and cover. You move at full speed inside your gas while enemies are slowed. This completely reverses the risk of operating in your own cloud and turns it into a one-sided information and speed advantage. Combined with Bangalore smoke, you have total visibility inside a combined gas/smoke zone while everyone else is completely blinded.
Push into your own gas aggressively — you're faster, you can see, they can't
Gas + Bangalore smoke = total blackout for enemies, orange highlights for you — this is the squad's best combo
Call highlighted positions immediately — "left side crouched behind box" changes how the squad plays it
Enemies in gas are taking damage and panicking — they move predictably, making them easier to track
Tactical Nox Gas Trap
Deploys a canister that triggers when an enemy approaches, releasing Nox Gas. You carry up to 6. Canisters have 75 HP — but a destroyed canister still releases its gas, so shooting them is never a clean answer. Gas slows enemies and deals stacking damage. Season 28 note: Hardlight Mesh can seal a doorway with a trap behind it, creating an enclosed gas chamber with no safe entry point.
Place at door frames, not room centers — forces a choice between gas exposure or shooting the canister (and getting gassed anyway)
Stack 2–3 canisters at main entries — damage and slow stack, making the push extremely costly
Place one canister elevated on a shelf or crate — harder to spot and shoot, activates independently
Traps before the fight, never during — the window closes the moment enemies arrive at the door
Controller class perk: hit the ring console during setup — free ring intel while your canisters go down
S28: Hardlight Mesh + canister on the far side of a doorway = enemies cannot safely enter or clear it
Ultimate Nox Gas Grenade
A large thrown grenade that creates an instant, wide-radius gas cloud on impact. No setup required — immediate area denial at range. The cloud persists long enough to force repositioning or punish enemies caught mid-rotation. Works alone or layered on top of your existing trap network to seal exits and create a complete gas box.
Throw into the space enemies are moving toward, not where they are — deny the destination
Into a building before the squad pushes = enemies are slowed and highlighted before anyone enters
Final ring: throw at the approach corridor where ring forces rotation — they walk directly into it
Use it proactively — it shapes the fight before it starts, don't save it for panic situations
You see everything in the cloud, they don't — push immediately after the grenade lands
Legend Perks — Know Your Options
How Perks Work
Evo armor levels unlock one perk per tier — two choices per match. Pick based on whether you're anchoring a building or playing mobile rotation denial that game.
Perk — Tier 1 Nox Extend
Increases gas duration on all Nox sources — traps and grenade linger longer. Extends the hold window and maximizes damage per canister deployed.
Best when anchoring — enemies can't wait out the gas if it persists significantly longer
Perk — Tier 1 (Alt) Extra Traps
Increases Nox Gas Trap carry capacity. More canisters means more buildings covered or denser layering at a single position when committing to a hold.
Best when rotating between positions — trap multiple structures before final ring without running dry
Perk — Tier 2 Toxic Rain
Nox Gas Grenade releases additional small canisters on impact — massively increases the spread and area of the gas zone created.
Best for open areas and rotation corridors — nearly impossible to skirt around the expanded spread
Perk — Tier 2 (Alt) Gas Overflow
Destroyed Nox Gas Traps release a larger cloud than normal — punishes the only reliable way to safely clear your canisters before entry.
Best when enemies are likely to shoot your traps — turns their safe play into a bigger problem than the trap itself
Recommended for Tarbo
Extra Traps + Toxic Rain for mobile denial — more coverage, bigger ult footprint in final rings. Nox Extend + Gas Overflow for dedicated hold games — maximum durability on your setup and maximum punishment for pushes.
Season 28 Context — Breach
Hardlight Mesh Changes the Game for Caustic
Hardlight Mesh creates destructible wall barriers that can block doorways, windows, and corridors. For Caustic, this is significant: a Mesh placed in a doorway with a Nox Gas Trap behind it seals the entry — enemies must break the Mesh first, and the moment they do, they step directly into triggered gas. Breaking Mesh makes noise, so you hear the push before they're through.
▸ Mesh + canister = enemies must commit to breaking the seal before knowing what's inside
▸ Stack Mesh panels to buy more setup time in chaotic early contact
▸ Gas doesn't harm squadmates — Bangalore and Alter push freely through your own cloud
▸ Destroyed canisters still gas — shooting a trap is never a clean answer
▸ Nox Vision + Bangalore smoke = full blackout/full visibility — strongest combined zone in S28
Trap Craft — Core Principle
Setup Before Contact — Always
Every scenario below only works if canisters are placed before enemies arrive. The window between landing and first contact is your setup window. Use all of it. Call "setting up" out loud so Bangalore and Alter know to buy you time rather than push.
The One Rule Enemies Can't Win Against
A destroyed canister still releases gas. Enemies have no clean answer — push through gas, shoot the canister and get gassed, or don't push at all. Every trap is a problem with no good solution. The only counter to Caustic is avoiding the zone entirely, which means they're ceding space to you.
Building Hold Setups
Single-Entry Lock
When: Structure with 1–2 entry points, squad committing to hold
  • T1Door frame canister — hinge side, low, hardest angle to see and shoot from outside
  • T2Second canister 1m inside the same entry — overlapping gas zones, stacked slow and damage on anyone who gets through T1
  • T3Elevated canister on a shelf or crate inside the doorway — activates independently from a harder-to-destroy angle
  • T4Window canister if one exists — no free peeks, forces full commitment to a covered approach
  • T5–T6Interior chokepoint or stairwell — enemies who breach still hit gas before reaching your squad
Any push costs significant HP, slows the entry, and highlights everyone immediately through Nox Vision — call positions the moment the gas triggers
Multi-Entry Building
When: Larger structure with 3+ ways in — prioritize coverage over density
  • T1–T2Main door — 2 stacked canisters, this is the most likely push angle, make it the most expensive
  • T3Secondary door — 1 canister minimum, enough to trigger Nox Vision and slow the secondary push
  • T4Window — 1 canister, eliminates the free peek angle
  • T5–T6Interior hallway or room center — enemies who breach still can't move freely inside without hitting more gas
Every entry costs something. No room inside the building is safe either — enemies who break through are still in a gas zone with no clean position
Rotation Denial
Corridor Seal
When: No building available — ring forces enemies through a natural choke
  • T1Canister at the narrowest point — can't be walked past without triggering
  • T2Second canister 3–4m back in the same corridor — catches teams who rush through the first
  • T3Side angle canister at a rock or cover frame — catches teams hugging the wall to avoid the center trap
  • ULTHold the Nox Gas Grenade for when the ring actually closes — deploy it at the corridor the moment pressure builds, not before
Teams who need this path take heavy damage getting through or burn time finding an alternate route — both outcomes are wins for your ring position
Open Ring — Gas Perimeter
When: Final ring is in the open, no structure to anchor
  • T1–T2Canisters at the two closest approach angles to your squad — defines the engagement boundary
  • T3Canister at the nearest cover piece between you and the closest enemy squad — punishes that position
  • T4–T5Flank approaches — don't leave obvious wide angles unguarded, even one canister forces a decision
  • ULTThrow into the ring corridor where the final zone will force everyone to rotate through — deploy as ring closes
No walls needed — gas defines the perimeter the same way a building does. Teams approaching from any direction pay a cost. You've made open terrain your territory.
Hardlight Mesh Combos (S28)
Sealing the Entry
Place a Nox Gas Trap behind a doorway, then place Hardlight Mesh in the door frame. Enemies cannot enter without destroying the Mesh — the moment it breaks, they step directly into triggered gas. The Mesh break is audible, giving you advance warning of the push before they're even through.
▸ Mesh buys 3–5 extra seconds of setup time in early contact situations
▸ Stack multiple Mesh panels in a hallway to create a real delay layer
▸ The gas canister behind the Mesh is invisible until the Mesh is already breaking
▸ Even if enemies destroy both — they took gas damage and you heard it coming
Squad Identity
The Trio — Bangalore · Caustic · Alter
This squad wins by controlling where fights happen — not by chasing them. Bangalore sets the tempo with smokes and buys setup time. You define which spaces are safe and which aren't — trapping entries before any enemy arrives. Alter creates secondary angles and extraction around your position. Every piece multiplies the others: smokes hide the approach, gas seals the interior, portals provide the escape. Teams that push into this have no clean option.
The Setup — Entry Trap Sequence
Lock the Building
Call: "Setting up — hold the outside"
  • TarboGoes inside immediately and traps all entries — main door, secondary door, windows. Full build-out before any contact. 2 stacked on the primary entry, 1 on everything else. Calls "set" when done.
  • BangaloreHolds exterior. If anyone pushes during setup, throws smoke at the approach — buys the 20–30 seconds Tarbo needs to finish the build.
  • AlterPlaces Void Passage inside the building giving the squad an exit through a non-trapped route. Sets Nexus now — before the fight starts, not when the hold breaks.
Timing Is Everything
Traps only work if they're placed before the push. Bangalore's job is to protect the setup window. Alter's job is to set the escape before the fight is hot. Once Tarbo calls "set," the building is yours — everyone defends from position.
Push Combo — Taking a Position
Gas First, Squad Second
Call: "Smoke push — gas inside, go"
  • BangaloreSmokes the outside approach — kills enemy vision of the push while the squad closes distance
  • TarboThrows Nox Gas Grenade into the target position before the squad enters. Pushes in behind it — Nox Vision shows highlighted enemies through the cloud. Calls positions out loud immediately.
  • AlterPlaces Void Passage as a secondary entry angle or emergency extraction layer while the push is live
Why This Works
The grenade goes in first — enemies are slowed and highlighted before anyone enters. Bangalore smoke covers the approach so they don't see the push coming. Tarbo enters with full Nox Vision and calls what he sees. Bangalore and Alter play off those calls. This is the combo that turns the passive from a solo advantage into a squad-wide intelligence edge.
Late Game Protocol
Ring 3 and Beyond
Late game Caustic is about identifying the right building and claiming it before you're forced to move. The squad that doesn't have to rotate under pressure in the final ring almost always wins. When ring 2 closes, identify the most defensible structure in the likely final ring zone — get there early, trap it before anyone else arrives.
▸ Claim the building before ring 3 — setup needs time, not urgency
▸ Alter places Nexus when the building is taken — not when the hold breaks
▸ Ult grenade covers the approach corridor when ring starts closing — forces rotaters through gas
▸ Bangalore smokes the exterior — they're blind coming in, Tarbo sees everything inside
▸ Call Nox Vision highlights out loud — it's the squad's biggest late-game information edge
Open Ring Adaptation
If final ring is in the open, pivot to the gas perimeter setup: traps at the two closest approach angles, ult grenade into the rotation corridor as ring closes. Same control principle — no walls required.
